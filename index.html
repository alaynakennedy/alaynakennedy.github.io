Hi, I'm Alayna!

I'm an AI enthusiast intersting in creating ethical algorithms and aligning human and machine values.

Currently working as a data scientist in IBM's public sector practice, I make predictive models for fraud detection, improve natural language processing models for job matching, and create tools to help other data scientists use AI ethically.

I graduated from Penn State University with a Bachelors in Engineering Science. I also wrote and published an undergraduate honors thesis on using stories to teach computers human values.

Check out my resume or shoot me an email if you'd like to know more!

INTERESTS





Machine Learning

I've always been driven by curiosity. It's what drew me to engineering, and what made me fall in love with machine learning after a summer fellowship studying neural networks. I delight in the cleverness, context, and artistry of using a well-implemented model to solve a complex problem.


AI Ethics

Concerned with the dearth of AI safety and ethics work, I wrote my undergraduate thesis on a solution to the AI value alignment problem. Since then, I've read a ton of AI ethics work, developed an AI ethics strategy for IBM’s public sector practice, and implemented a tool to help data scientists use AI ethically.


Policy

During college, I took a philosophy of technology policy class and was immediately hooked. Since then, I’ve been fascinated by the intersection of technology, philosophy, and governance. Since moving to D.C. I’ve become a regular at every think tank and policy discussion surrounding AI.


Effective Altruism

My two most important core values are meaningful work and intellectual engagement; I found both in Effective Altruism community. Like most EA’s, I’m passionate about advocating for important causes like AI policy and about doing the right thing for the long-term future of humanity.
