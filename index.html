Hi, I'm Alayna!

I'm an AI ethics consultant with experience in industry, policy, and academia. My interests and work focus on the ways the private sector is implementing "tech ethics" policies and procedures.

Currently pursuing a master's in Science and Technology in Society at the University of Edinburgh, I'm studying the power dynamics and social worlds of industry, academia, and government with regards to the emerging tech ethics ecosystem. Before that, I worked on IBM's AI Ethics Board to create tools to help other data scientists use AI ethically.

I graduated from Penn State University with a Bachelors in Engineering Science. I also wrote and published an undergraduate honors thesis on using stories to teach computers human values.

Check out my resume or shoot me an email if you'd like to know more!

INTERESTS





Industry Trends in Tech Ethics

In my 3 years at IBM, I worked in Washington DC on industry and government development of technology. There, I started an AI ethics group to assess the risk of public sector AI deliverables. I continued on to work with the IBM AI ethics board on corporate research and education initiatives about tech ethics for design, bias mitigation techniques, and many other corporate AI ethics trends. Even after leaving IBM, I'm still interested in the ways companies attempt to implement tech ethics through a framework of corporate social responsibility.


Power & AI

My master's research, informed by my past industry experience, examines the different social worlds of industry, government, and academia and how they interact and compete within the realm of tech ethics. I am particularly concerned with questions of power and the political economy of digital technology.


Tech & Society

As an undergraduate, I studied engineering and machine learning. As a current master's student, I'm focusing on the sociology of technology - how society and technology co-create each other, and the complex ways that they interact. I'm particularly interested in the social impacts of digitial technologies, and how companies and governments can mitigate harmful societal effects of emerging tech.


Engagement with the Tech Ethics Community

Tech ethics, as an emerging field, relies on community and activism. I've been involved with community projects with All Tech is Human, the Montreal AI Ethics Institute, and the organizing group No Tech for Tyrants.

PROJECTS

AI Governance Writer

I worked with IBM’s internal government think tank to publish two blogs and a longer report on the effects of AI in the government industry. With the Partnership for Public Service, we hosted roundtables of CXOs from across federal agencies to discuss AI policy and adoption. I took notes at these events and used the discussion as the basis for the blogs I wrote. I learned a great deal about the challenges government agencies face in the day-to-day work of implementing AI tools and policies in these discussions!

NLP Developer

As a part of an IBM project, I worked on a tool that uses Natural Language Processing (NLP) to perform job matching of employees across government agencies. Government desperately needs to retool their workforce to address emerging technology needs, but they can’t find people to fill positions in the missing middle of human-computer interaction. Initially developed for the Department of Labor, the prototype I helped develop would help all government agencies match job descriptions of new positions to internal resumes, using personality insights and conceptual dictionaries.

AI Ethics Strategist

With one of my fellow analysts, I helped launch an initiative in IBM’s public sector practice to address issues of AI ethics and bias. We read copious amounts of papers on AI governance and ethics and created a rubric for data analysts to use when developing models. Our goal was to identify which algorithms were higher risk (an unexplainable neural net being riskier than a decision tree), and what types of data required a closer look by a social scientist (race information, gender, etc.)

AI Newsletter Author

I work with the leader of IBM’s Defense and Intelligence (D&I) practice to inform him about the specific AI ethics and bias concerns that exist within the D&I community. I love reading about this topic, and I have a knack for summarizing information, so I started writing a weekly newsletter about developments in the AI research, policy, and ethics space that were relevant to the D&I industry. The newsletter was disseminated internally within IBM, as well as externally to our DoD and Intelligence Agency clients.

Board Member

In college, I helped start Nittany Data Labs, Penn State’s first data science organization, which taught introductory Python to students and partnered with industry sponsors like Nestle and Disney on data science projects. I served as a lead on a 3-month Python bootcamp for new members, as well as leading teams during our annual data science case competitions. As the only woman on the board, I was especially passionate about recruiting and retaining other undergraduate women to our organization, since I firmly believe that the tech world needs more diversity to reach its full potential.
